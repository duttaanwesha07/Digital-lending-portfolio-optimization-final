# Workstream A — Engagement Charter

## Why this workstream exists

Before anyone touches data or writes code, the team has to agree on what we are actually solving. Without a written charter, every later workstream risks drifting in a direction the client never asked for, and we end up with answers to questions nobody posed. This workstream forces that alignment up front.

## What this workstream does

The team has uninamously agreed upon what is in scope, what is explicitly out of scope, how default and delinquency are defined, and which key questions the engagement must answer. The output is captured in a charter that becomes the single reference point for the rest of the project.

## What it produces

A signed-off engagement charter document.

## How it fits with other workstreams

Feeds Workstream B (data design) by defining what data is needed to answer the questions. Feeds Workstreams I, J, and K (recommendations, report, deck) as the storyline spine — they all reference back to "what did we promise we would answer?"
