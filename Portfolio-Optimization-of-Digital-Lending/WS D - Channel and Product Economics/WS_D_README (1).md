# Workstream D — Channel and Product Economics

## Why this workstream exists

The engagement charter asks which channels and which products are profitable, and which are bleeding money. Without this analysis, the team cannot recommend stopping (or scaling) any particular channel — they simply do not know what each channel is contributing or costing the book.

## What this workstream does

Breaks down the loan book by acquisition channel and by product type. For each combination of channel and product, computes revenue, losses, customer acquisition cost, and servicing cost to arrive at a per-loan net contribution. Identifies which channels and products are loss-makers and which are profit centres. Produces supporting visuals (charts, heatmaps) that make the findings explainable to non-analytical readers.

## What it produces

A channel-economics workbook, supporting visualisations, and a clear finding on which channels and products to deprioritise or scale.

## How it fits with other workstreams

Feeds Workstream G — the channel and product findings become candidate policy levers to evaluate at baseline and under macro stress. Also feeds Workstream I (recommendations) directly when the channel finding becomes one of the supporting levers in the final recommendation.
