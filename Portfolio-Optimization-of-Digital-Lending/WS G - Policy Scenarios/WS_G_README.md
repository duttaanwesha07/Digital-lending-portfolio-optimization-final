# Workstream G — Policy Scenarios

## Why this workstream exists

Workstreams D and E identify the problematic slices of the book — which channels are loss-making, which segments contain the bad apples. But identifying a problem is not the same as knowing the impact of fixing it. Workstream G converts each candidate policy fix from an idea into a costed number: what does the book gain (or lose) if this lever is actually applied, and does that gain hold up if the macro environment turns?

## What this workstream does

Defines each candidate lever as a precise rule that an underwriting system could execute — for example, "decline applicants where the credit grade is C, D, or E AND cashflow consistency is below a specific threshold." For each lever, computes the impact on book contribution at baseline and under macro stress (modelled as higher-than-expected default rates). Filters out levers that don't survive stress — defensive levers that fall apart in bad macro conditions aren't useful. The strongest surviving lever becomes the candidate for the headline recommendation; the next strongest become supporting levers.

## What it produces

A scenarios table showing each lever's volume impact and net contribution impact at each macro condition. This becomes the analytical foundation of the recommendation.

## How it fits with other workstreams

Consumes Workstreams D and E. Hands off to Workstream H (sensitivity analysis), which stress-tests the chosen levers against assumption ranges, and Workstream I (recommendations), which packages the chosen levers for the client.
