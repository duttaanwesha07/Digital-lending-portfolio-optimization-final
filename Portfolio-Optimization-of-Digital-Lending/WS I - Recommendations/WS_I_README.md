# Workstream I — Recommendations

## Why this workstream exists

The CRO does not read scenario tables or sensitivity matrices — they read a clear, prioritised recommendation with named actions, named owners, and named risks. Workstream I converts everything the analytical workstreams produced into a structured recommendation document that can be presented to leadership without further translation.

## What this workstream does

Selects the headline lever and the supporting levers from the scenarios produced by Workstream G. For each lever, names the implementation owner (which team inside the lender actually does the work), defines a guardrail metric (how anyone will know whether the lever is working as expected), lays out the implementation timeline, and identifies the major risks and mitigations. Layers in the sensitivity commentary from Workstream H to demonstrate that the recommendation is robust.

## What it produces

A structured recommendation document with executive summary, strategic context, headline lever, supporting levers, combined portfolio impact, implementation roadmap, risks, sensitivity commentary, and an assumptions appendix.

## How it fits with other workstreams

Consumes every analytical workstream — D (channel economics), E (segmentation), F (EWS), G (scenarios), H (sensitivity). Feeds Workstreams J (report) and K (deck), which take the same recommendation content and package it for written and presentational delivery to the client.
